## Amortized Bayesian disease mapping and boundary detection

**Paper:** Luca Aiello and Sudipto Banerjee. "Spatial Disease Mapping and
Disparity Detection Using Generative AI: An Amortized Bayesian Learning
Framework." arXiv preprint, 2026.

**Publication date:** June 16, 2026, per arXiv submission metadata.

**Source:** [arXiv:2606.18146](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18146)

**Modeling approach:** The paper reports an amortized Bayesian framework for
areal disease mapping and spatial boundary detection across graphs with
different numbers of regions and adjacency structures. The model combines a
Poisson count likelihood, offsets, covariates, a rule for interrupting smoothing
across dissimilar neighboring areas, and a directed acyclic graph autoregressive
(DAGAR) residual spatial prior. Posterior inference is approximated by a neural
engine trained on simulated maps: a permutation-invariant graph-aware summary
network encodes counts, offsets, covariates, and adjacency matrices, and a
conditional normalizing flow generates approximate posterior draws.

**Key finding:** The abstract reports accurate parameter recovery,
near-nominal interval coverage, calibrated posterior predictive behavior,
informative posterior boundary probabilities, and close agreement with MCMC on
primary boundary evidence. It also reports applications to Glasgow respiratory
disease and California lung cancer maps, where one trained neural engine was
deployed across distinct real-world areal graphs and returned boundary
conclusions consistent with localized smoothing analyses.

**Why it matters:** Spatial disease mapping workflows are often fitted one map
at a time with MCMC or deterministic approximations. A reusable amortized
posterior engine could make boundary-aware Bayesian mapping faster for repeated
surveillance, sensitivity analysis, and multi-region comparison, while retaining
uncertainty summaries such as boundary probabilities and posterior predictive
checks.

**Caution on details:** The technical notation in the figure is explanatory and
may differ from the paper. The source abstract identifies the Poisson
likelihood, DAGAR residual prior, covariate-driven smoothing interruption,
permutation-invariant summary network, conditional normalizing flow, simulation
training, MCMC comparison, and the two applied disease-mapping examples; finer
parameterization details should be checked against the full preprint before
implementation.
